GOLDEN HOUR : Part.5


San
"What did it just say?"

San asked the others, already prepared to channel electricity through the water if things went awry. The creature's pronunciation was slurred, and no one could fully make sense of what it was saying. No one except Woo Young.

Woo Young
"'Mother bird...'"

Jong Ho
"Hyung, hold the hose steady!"

As Woo Young quietly repeated Sopro's words to himself, his grasp faltered slightly and the fire hose began to thrash like a wriggling eel. At Jong Ho's warning, Yun Ho reacted a split second faster, grabbing the wildly writhing hose. Startled, Woo Young hastily caught hold of it again as well.

San
"I don't think water alone is going to be enough."

The water seemed to do little more than wash away the sludge that held the Frankenstein monster together. It wasn't doing much damage. As its earthen skin was stripped away, the Frankenstein monster's muscles and skeletal fame were laid bare.

'Mother bird. Why did Sopro call us that?'
Lost in thought and unable to focus on the attack, Woo Young found his thoughts drifting. He was suddenly reminded of something the scholar they had met through Yun Ho had once said : The nature of this stone is like a child, and the king in possesstion of this stone becomes the parent of that child. Therefore, if the king has an evil heart, the child will learn that wickedness, and if the king has a good heart, it will learn goodness.

Sopro was like a child. From that perspective, everything it had done began to make sense. A child, guided more by curiosity than judgement, reaches out first, driven by instinct rather than reason because they have so little experience of the world. And it is through those very experiences that they begin to learn how the world works.

If Sopro was a child, then the very first emotion it felt upon entering this world must have been wonder, followed by a curiosity to experience more. From joy and happiness to sorrow and dispair, from shyness and embarrassment to shame and fear, and even to terror and anger, its emotions must have stretched outward in every direction. And along the way, it would inevitably have been wounded. Hurt and marked by a world that had once seemed so new and beautiful.

'Mother bird. Mother bird.' As Woo Young tried to see things from Sopro's perspective, it finally dawned on him that Sopro had been calling him the mother bird. The one who had brought it into this world. The first person it had encoundered upon emerging into it. The one who had made if feel joy, happines, and excitement for the very first time.

Hong Joong
"It's coming this way!"

Min Gi
"San!"

San started the engine of his food truck. Grabbing the long cable he had already hooked up to the battery, he printed toward Seong Hwa.

San
"Everyone's wearing boots, right?!"

The moment he heard their answers, San thrust the cable into the gushing stream of water. The current surged along the water and the creature, circling its entire body. 'Uoaaaah-!' It let out a pained groan.

Woo Young
"It worked!"

They might not be able to kill it, but it seemed possible to knock it out for a moment. The members let out a sigh of releaf. Then it happened: The food truck's engine suddenly died. The electricity racing along the water cut out all at once. 'What...? There's no way that thing has allies.' As one, their eyes snapped towards the drivers seat of the food truck.

Woo Young
"Everyone, stop."

Woo Young shouted as he turned to the others. Sometimes in the chaos, he has rushed over to the food truck and turned off the engine. Deciding they had to attack before the creature recovered, Yeo Sang restarted the engine. But almost immediately, Woo Young shut it off again. Yeo Sang looked at him, his face etched with confusion.

Yun Ho
"Are you out of your mind? Or did Sopro sync with you or something?"

Woo Young
"My mind is clearer than it's ever been."

Woo Young answered with a firm, resolute expression.

Woo Young
"Jong Ho can you shut the valve too?"

Jong Ho
"Huh? No-if we do that, then that thing-"

Woo Young
"Please. I have something in mind."

At Woo Young's unwavering insistence, Jong Ho hesitated only a moment before closing the fire hose valve. The water stopped, and with it, the electric current. Fortunately, the creature was still too overwhelmed with pain to regain its senses.
Woo Young
"I know it's hard to trust me after causing something this big... but just this once, trust me."

Woo Young connected his phone to the speaker attached to San's food truck. Soon, music began to flow through the mud-soaked village. It was that song, the one the members had recorded togethr for the first and last time after returning to World A.

Seong Hwa
"There's no way something covered in corpses and mud would have memories or emotions. And there's no way it would react to this music we've never even released into the world."

As the creature began to recover from the pain, Seong Hwa, growing anxious, pressed Woo Young. Jong Ho, who had kept a hand on the valve, moved to turn it again. At the same time, San signaled to Min Gi, who stood beside the food truck, to start the engine.

Yeo Sang
"Let's trust him."

Watching Woo Young's trembling hands, shaking as his fingers dug into his palms, Yeo Sang spoke to the others. Normally the one who nagged the most, Yeo Sang's words suprised even Woo Young. In truth, he has often thought Yeo Sang was the one who trusted him the least.

Yeo Sang
"Jung Woo Young might act a little recless sometimes, but he's always taken responsibility for his actions."

Yeo Sang firmly gripped one of Woo Young's shoulders, as if urging him to go through with what he had decided, and then stepped away from the food truck.

Hong Joong
"If it doesn't work, turn the valve immediately, Jong Ho. And Woo Young, you start the engine."

Woo Young nodded once in gratitude. Then he tossed the car keys to Min Gi.

Woo Young
"If things go sideways, you might have to start the engine. Stay right here and don't go anywhere."

Min Gi
"What? What are you planning?"

Instead of answering, Woo Young raised the volume of the music. Now freed from the pain, Sopro turned toward the food truck where the music was playing. And in front of it stood Woo Young. Facing the towering Sopro, Woo Young looked impossibly small.

Woo Young
"Do you remember? This song."

Without warning, Woo Young spoke to Sopro. The others, worried that Sopro might charge at him, prepared to attack at a moment's notice.

Woo Young
"After I brought you out into the world... this is the song I listened to on that first train home."

On his way home after awakening Sopro, Woo Young had listened to this song. At the time, he described it, was real. But when that red light scattered and filled Yeo Sang's villa, he felt a certainty, a belief that his dreams might finally come true.

He believed that the desire to sing and dance together again whith the members would reach them. His heart had raced with a vague hope that he might relive that same exhilarating experience of overwhelming camaraderie again. So, as he watched the sky gradually brighten, he boarded the first train home and listened to the last song they had sung together. Sunlight had poured into the train carriage, filling it with a gentle warmth, and through the headset covering his ears flowed a soft melody that seemed to match the moment perfectly. He hadn't realized it then, but Sopro must have already been inside him, sharing in that feeling and memory.

Listening to the music, Sopro blinked slowly, as if memories and emotions buried deep within her memories and emotions buried deep within her were beginningnto surface. Sopro twisted in anguish as she clutched her head. When Sopro had inhabited human bodies before, and they had gagged while recalling certain memories, Sopro had only felt a vague, instinctual urge not to be expelled. She had never understood the emotions those memories stirred. But now, the memories rising within Sopro were her own.

When she had first been freed from that unbearably small world and was held in the embrace of her 'mother bird', she had looked out at the dazzling world through his eyes and listened to that same warm music. Though it wasn't even truly her own head, it felt as if it were burning. A wave of nausea rose, but there was nothing to throw up. She and her body were already bound as one. To expel Sopro would be the same as casting out her own heart.

Yun Ho
"Did it work?"

He studied Sopro, her body hunched over, head buried between her arms. She looked just like the others had before regaining their senses. But then-

Hong Joong
"Jong Ho!"

The moment of hesitation broke. Sopro, now even more enraged than before, charged at Woo Young with terrifying speed. Alarmed, Hong Joong signaled to Jong Ho, and Jong Ho immediately opened the water valve. San called out to Min Gi, who had frozen in shock, and the moment Min Gi started the engine, they heard Yeo Sang yell.

Yeo Sang
"Jung Woo Young! Woo Young, don't!"

But Woo Young was already moving, running toward Sopro as it charged straight at him. Yeo Sang ran to grab him, but it was too late. Sopro opened her massive jaws and swallowed Woo Young whole. It was over in an instant, with no chance to attempt another attack with water and electricity.

San
"Turn it off, Min Gi."

They couldn't bring themselves to try another attack when there was a chance Woo Young might get caught in the blast. Everything had happened in the blink of an eye. They were all frozen in terror.

Yeo Sang
"No... no!"

Yeo Sang had always shown his affection by picking on Woo Young. At first, it had been out of embarrassment, but over time it had become a habit. His words often came out sharper than intended, and he often regretted them afterward. This bad habit of Yeo Sang's reared its head on that day, too, when they had all gathered at his villa for the first time in a long while.

When Woo Young suggested that they try to get back together as a group again, saying that they hadn't truly gone all way yet, Yeo Sang had instinctively pushed back. Thinking of the members who had spent three years building new lives, his words had come out harsher than intended.

He could picture it all too clearly, the hardships they must have faced while carving out new paths, the ways they would have had to steady themselves, convenience themselves to keep going, and push through it day after day. And so, he pretended not to understand Woo Young's feelings, because it was easier that way. Even though, deep down, he understood them all too well. That night, the night Woo Young awakened Sopro, Yeo Sang had seen everything. He had woken up thirsty, went for a sip of water, and was on his way back to bed when he witnessed it. He'd seen Woo Young released Sopro and make a desperate wish. And yet, Yeo Sang had not stopped him.

Because, in truth, a part of him missed that time, as well. Perhaps he simply didn't want to admit that he carried the same lingering regrets that Woo Young did. Perhaps he had dismissed the as a childish impulse, forcing them down. Maybe that was why he always made such sharp remarks whenever Woo Young's words struck a chord, reaching something real within him.

And now, Yeo Sang felt it was far too late, leaving him to drown in regret. For the first time, he had shown his trust in Woo Young, only for Woo Young to be put in danger because of it. He had never told him properly how greteful he was, how much he cared for him, and how simply having him by his side gave him more strength than he could ever put into words. He couldn't lose him like this.

Without a second thought, Yeo Sng ran toward Sopro to save Woo Young. Sopro roared, kicking up clouds of dust and dirt into the air. The mud and debris were swept up by the wind, swirling violently, and forming a massive vortex. It coiled around Sopro, expanding as it spun, growing larger wwith every passing moment.

Seong Hwa
"Grab Yeo Sang!"

Min Gi caught Yeo Sang as he was being pulled into the vortex. Seong Hwa grabbed Min Gi's arm. Hong Joong seized Seong Hwa's hand, Yun Ho held onto Hong Joong, Jong Ho gripped Yun Ho, and San latched onto Jong Ho. But the vortex, now like a ranging tornado, swallowed them all without mercy.

Hong Joong
"Don't let go no matter what!"

With those final words, the members disappeared into the storm.
